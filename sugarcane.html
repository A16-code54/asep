Early detection for high-yield sugar recovery

Fungal

Red Rot

Often called the "Cancer of Sugarcane," it causes massive loss in sugar recovery.

Symptoms:
  • Yellowing and drooping of the third or fourth leaf.
  • Internal cane tissue turns red with white cross-patches.
  • Alcoholic smell from split-open canes.
AI Feature:

Hyperspectral imaging to detect internal tissue decay before external yellowing occurs.

Fungal

Sugarcane Smut

Caused by Sporisorium scitamineum, it spreads quickly in ratoon crops.

Symptoms:
  • Production of a long, black, whip-like structure from the top.
  • Stunted growth with thin stalks.
  • Excessive tillering (grass-like appearance).
AI Feature:

Drone-based thermal monitoring to identify patches of stunted ratoon growth.

Phytoplasma

Grassy Shoot

A disease transmitted by aphids that stops cane formation entirely.

Symptoms:
  • Numerous small, thin, yellowish-white shoots.
  • Dense bunch of shoots giving a "grassy" appearance.
  • Plants fail to form millable stalks.
AI Feature:

Aphid population tracking and predictive alerts based on humidity spikes.

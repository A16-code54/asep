Fungal

Blister Blight

Caused by Exobasidium vexans, it targets young succulent leaves and buds.

Symptoms:
  • Small, pale green/yellow spots on young leaves.
  • Circular white "blisters" on the underside of leaves.
  • Curled and distorted leaves that turn black and die.
AI Feature:

Automated leaf-curl analysis to detect the early stages of infection before blisters burst.

Fungal

Grey Blight

Common in mature leaves, it significantly reduces the quality of the tea harvest.

Symptoms:
  • Greyish-brown patches with concentric rings on leaves.
  • Tiny black dots (fruiting bodies) appear on the patches.
  • Patches often start from the leaf tip or margins.
AI Feature:

Spectroscopic analysis of leaf color to estimate the impact on chlorophyll and tannin content.

Algal

Red Rust

Unlike most rusts, this is caused by algae and affects low-vigor plants.

Symptoms:
  • Orange-red, velvety patches on the upper leaf surface.
  • Cracking of the bark on infected stems.
  • General yellowing of the entire tea bush.
AI Feature:

Soil health monitoring to identify nutrient deficiencies that make bushes susceptible to algal rust.

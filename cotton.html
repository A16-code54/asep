Cotton Protection Clinic

Safeguard your 'White Gold' from devastating pests and diseases

Soil-borne

Fusarium Wilt

A deadly fungal infection that attacks the plant's vascular system, blocking water flow.

Symptoms:
  • Yellowing and browning of leaf edges.
  • Wilting of the plant even when soil is moist.
  • Black/brown streaks inside the stem when cut.
AI Feature:

Soil temperature and pH analysis to assess the risk of fungal spread in the field.

Bacterial

Black Arm / Blight

Commonly known as "Anguliyak" in India, it affects leaves, stems, and bolls.

Symptoms:
  • Water-soaked, angular spots on leaves.
  • Blackened lesions on the stem (Black Arm phase).
  • Premature dropping of cotton bolls.
AI Feature:

Image-based detection of angular spots to differentiate from nutrient deficiency.

Major Pest

Pink Bollworm

The most significant threat to Indian cotton, damaging the fiber quality and yield.

Symptoms:
  • Rosetted flowers (petals twisted together).
  • Small entry holes in developing bolls.
  • Damaged lint and stained cotton seeds.
AI Feature:

Pheromone trap data integration to alert farmers of peak moth activity.

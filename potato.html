Potato Disease Clinic

Monitoring tuber quality and preventing blight outbreaks

High Alert

Late Blight

Caused by Phytophthora infestans, it is the most destructive disease of potatoes worldwide.

Symptoms:
  • Water-soaked, dark green to black spots on leaves.
  • White "cottony" fungal growth on leaf undersides during humid weather.
  • Tubers show reddish-brown dry rot internally.
AI Feature:

Weather-based forecasting (Smith Periods) that alerts farmers 48 hours before the infection spreads.

Fungal

Early Blight

Caused by Alternaria solani, it typically affects older leaves first as the plant matures.

Symptoms:
  • Small, brown spots with "target-board" concentric rings.
  • Yellow halo surrounding the brown lesions.
  • Premature leaf drop and reduced tuber size.
AI Feature:

Automated image recognition to distinguish "target spots" from nutrient deficiencies like Potassium.

Bacterial/Soil

Common Scab

A soil-borne bacterial disease that affects the skin of the potato, reducing its market value.

Symptoms:
  • Rough, corky, raised or pitted lesions on tuber skin.
  • Symptoms only appear on the tubers (underground).
  • Often occurs in soils with high pH (alkaline).
AI Feature:

Soil pH and moisture mapping to identify field zones at high risk for scab development.

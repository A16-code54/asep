Fungal

Purple Blotch

One of the most destructive leaf diseases, caused by Alternaria porri.

Symptoms:
  • Small, water-soaked lesions that turn purple.
  • Concentric rings visible within the purple spots.
  • Tips of leaves die back as spots enlarge.
AI Feature:

Color-segmentation analysis to calculate the percentage of leaf area infected for precise fungicide application.

Fungal

Downy Mildew

Thrives in cool, moist conditions and spreads rapidly during winter (Rabi) crops.

Symptoms:
  • Fine, violet-grey fluffy growth on leaf surfaces.
  • Leaves turn pale green, then yellow, and eventually collapse.
  • Systemic infection leads to stunted bulb growth.
AI Feature:

Micro-climate sensing to alert farmers when dew-point and temperature levels create a risk for mildew.

Soil-borne

Basal Rot

A fungal disease that attacks the bulb roots, often resulting in total crop loss during storage.

Symptoms:
  • Yellowing and curving of leaves from the tip.
  • Root decay and white fungal growth at the base of the bulb.
  • Bulbs become soft and watery (rotting).
AI Feature:

Soil moisture and drainage analysis to prevent water-logging conditions that trigger root decay.

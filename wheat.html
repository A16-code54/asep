Wheat Disease Clinic

Protect your golden harvest from common wheat threats

Fungal

Yellow (Stripe) Rust

Caused by Puccinia striiformis, it thrives in cool, moist climates.

Symptoms:
  • Yellow stripes formed by small pustules.
  • Powdery yellow spores that rub off on skin.
  • Stunted growth and shrivelled grains.
AI Feature:

Satellite-based temperature monitoring to predict rust outbreaks in North India.

Seed-borne

Loose Smut

A systemic disease where healthy grains are replaced by black powder.

Symptoms:
  • Wheat heads emerge covered in black spores.
  • The black mass replaces the entire spikelet.
  • Spores are easily blown away by wind.
AI Feature:

Grain quality analysis through high-res imaging to detect early contamination.

Fungal

Powdery Mildew

Common in high-humidity areas and dense wheat stands.

Symptoms:
  • White, cottony patches on leaves and stems.
  • Patches turn gray or brown as they age.
  • Premature yellowing and death of leaves.
AI Feature:

Humidity and canopy density sensors to alert farmers of high-risk conditions.

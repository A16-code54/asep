Fungal

Coffee Leaf Rust

Caused by Hemileia vastatrix, this is the most globally significant coffee disease.

Symptoms:
  • Yellowish-orange powdery spots on the leaf underside.
  • Premature leaf drop (defoliation).
  • Reduced berry size and "die-back" of twigs.
AI Feature:

Early-stage spectral analysis to detect spore colonization before yellowing is visible to the naked eye.

Fungal

Black Rot (Koleroga)

Highly prevalent in the high-rainfall regions of the Western Ghats during monsoons.

Symptoms:
  • Blackening and rotting of leaves and berries.
  • Dead leaves remaining suspended by fungal threads.
  • Slimy appearance during continuous wet weather.
AI Feature:

Rainfall threshold alerts that predict "Koleroga" outbreaks based on cumulative moisture levels.

Major Pest

Coffee Berry Borer

A tiny beetle that bores into the berry to lay eggs, destroying the bean inside.

Symptoms:
  • Small pin-sized entry holes at the tip of the berry.
  • Blue-black discoloration of the bored berry.
  • Loss of weight and quality of the final coffee bean.
AI Feature:

Macro-image recognition to identify entry holes and estimate infestation percentage across the estate.

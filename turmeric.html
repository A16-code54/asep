Soil-borne

Rhizome Rot (Soft Rot)

Caused by Pythium aphanidermatum, it is the most devastating disease in turmeric.

Symptoms:
  • Yellowing of lower leaves spreading upwards.
  • Base of the pseudostem becomes soft and watery.
  • Root system decays, making the plant easy to pull out.
AI Feature:

Soil moisture sensors & predictive drainage analysis to identify rot-prone waterlogging zones.

Fungal

Leaf Spot

Caused by Colletotrichum capsici, common during high monsoon humidity.

Symptoms:
  • Elliptical brown spots with gray centers on leaves.
  • Spots enlarge and coalesce, drying out the leaf.
  • Decreased photosynthesis leads to smaller rhizomes.
AI Feature:

Automated leaf image scanning via mobile app to distinguish between nutrient deficiency and fungal spots.

Fungal

Leaf Blotch

Caused by Taphrina maculans, appearing as small, reddish-brown spots.

Symptoms:
  • Numerous small spots appearing on both leaf surfaces.
  • Leaves turn yellow and dry up prematurely.
  • Heavy infection gives the field a scorched appearance.
AI Feature:

Spectral imaging via drones to detect "scorched" patches before they are visible to the naked eye.
